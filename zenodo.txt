Temporal Shear: Resolving the High-Redshift Efficiency Crisis

JWST has revealed coherent anomalies at z > 5: star formation efficiencies exceeding ΛCDM limits, overmassive black holes, and stellar masses surpassing dynamical masses — all concentrated in the deepest gravitational potentials. This study tests whether a single violation of the isochrony axiom can account for a systematic fraction of all three. The Temporal Equivalence Principle (TEP), a chameleon-screened scalar-tensor theory, predicts enhanced proper time in unscreened potentials. With an external local Cepheid prior α₀ = 0.58 ± 0.16 and zero parameters tuned to JWST data, this single mechanism simultaneously predicts four distinct anomaly classes: it accounts for ~34% of the Red Monster efficiency excess, resolves the benchmark literature stellar-mass-function excess at z = 6–10 and 8/9 anomalous Labbé+2023 galaxies under the z-dependent correction, reduces the cosmic SFRD excess from 11.1× to 2.6× ΛCDM, and provides a purely kinematic mechanism (differential temporal shear) for overmassive black hole growth in Little Red Dots, with a real-data-derived regime-level dynamical-mass comparison matching the scale of the de Graaff et al. 2024 anomaly.

The primary empirical evidence comprises two lines across three surveys (N = 4,726), together with one ancillary spatial indication and one derived regime-level comparison: (L1) a dust–Γ_t correlation (ρ = +0.62, N = 1,283) with ΔAIC ≈ -4.8 against a mass-matched threshold; (L2 - ancillary spatial indication) a preferred JADES DR5 direct-mass morphology branch with four supportive structural proxies after mass+z control (r_half,F277W/F444W partial ρ = -0.256, Gini = +0.361, σ_star = +0.624, N = 384), while the resolved colour-gradient branch retains the raw mass trend ρ(M*, ∇Color) = -0.166 and a directionally supportive debiased sign test but remains non-decisive; (L3) mass–sSFR inversion at z > 7; (L4) a real-data-derived regime-level resolution of the stellar-to-dynamical mass crisis (M* > Mdyn) via comparison with published kinematic literature. The sSFR sign inversion (L3) is mathematically immune to the "mass-proxy" critique: while systematic mass-measurement errors might induce a monotonic bias, they cannot produce a sharp sign inversion at z = 7. Furthermore, L3 carries information orthogonal to dust: in UNCOVER, partial ρ(Γ_t, sSFR | dust) = -0.49 (p = 10^-18).

A key model-discriminating result is the Uniformity Paradox: the anomaly is not that massive galaxies at z > 8 are dusty, but that low-mass galaxies are not — in a pattern that tracks gravitational potential depth (ρ = +0.56). Any standard-physics resolution that tunes a time-uniform parameter (e.g. enhanced AGB yields) cannot reproduce this mass-dependent suppression: if dust production were maximally efficient everywhere, dust should be ubiquitous or track star formation, not potential depth. Only a mechanism that selectively suppresses effective time in shallow potentials — as TEP predicts via Γ_t ≪ 1 for low-mass halos — reproduces the observed gradient without additional parameters.

Fitted polynomials collapse cross-survey (R² = -6.4) while t_eff generalises without training (ρ = 0.60–0.80). Full CAMB Boltzmann integration is consistent with Planck constraints (σ₈ within 0.1σ). The three independent JWST surveys each confirm L1 individually above 5σ with no clustering correction required between surveys (CEERS: z = 6.6σ; UNCOVER: z = 10.2σ; COSMOS-Web: z = 21.4σ). A Fisher combination of the three photometric L1 confirmations gives χ² = 627.5, p = 2.8 × 10^-132, z = 24.4σ. The dedicated UNCOVER z > 8 validation battery independently passes all four targeted tests, including ρ(t_eff, A_V | t_cosmic) = +0.600 (p = 5.0 × 10^-29), confirming that effective time rather than raw cosmic age organizes the dust signal. The functional-form test further confirms that t_eff outperforms t_cosmic in all three surveys independently at >5σ (Steiger Z = 5.3, 6.7, 16.8), ruling out redshift ordering as the active variable. Across the broader JWST evidence package, dependence-adjusted multi-test combinations remain strongly supportive; because those omnibus figures depend on correction choice, the manuscript treats the three-survey photometric L1 replication as the headline statistic. The corrected JWST recovery gives α₀ = 0.548 ± 0.010 (dust, Pearson R²) and α₀ = 0.548 ± 0.010 (joint four-observable), both consistent with the external Cepheid prior at 0.2σ. The tighter JWST concordance interval reflects internal agreement among the high-redshift observables rather than a redefinition of the local prior.

Several additional supporting results extend the empirical picture. COSMOS2025 blank-field support remains strong in the raw dust relation, reaching ρ(Γ_t, E(B-V)) = +0.657 at z = 9–13 (N = 1,467), while the fully mass+redshift-controlled partial remains positive but modest (ρ = +0.074, p = 4.3 × 10^-3) and is therefore treated as auxiliary support rather than as a primary partial-correlation confirmation. The cross-survey temporal-ordering test is recovered independently in all three surveys, with Δρ_time = +0.605 (UNCOVER), +0.711 (CEERS), and +0.862 (COSMOS-Web). UNCOVER DR4 MegaScience is null at z < 7, turns positive in the z = 7–8 and z = 8–9 bins (ρ = +0.388 and +0.492), and returns to null at z = 9–12. The dedicated UNCOVER dust-prediction battery passes 4/4 targeted tests. The JADES mass-to-light proxy check gives ρ = +0.599 raw and partial ρ = +0.741 after redshift control. Explicit mass debiasing strengthens previously weak signals by ~1.5×–1.9×. Key limitations: (i) Γ_t is a deterministic function of photometric halo mass, so breaking the mass-proxy degeneracy definitively requires IFU velocity dispersions; (ii) the live DJA-based GOODS-S and Balmer replications are retained as supplementary spectroscopic checks rather than as part of the primary evidence count.
