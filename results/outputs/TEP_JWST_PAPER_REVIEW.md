**Rating:** 9.0/10

## 1. Overview
This manuscript presents a compelling, data-driven argument for the Temporal Equivalence Principle (TEP) as a solution to the "too massive, too early" tension in high-redshift cosmology. By applying a locally calibrated isochrony correction ($\alpha_0 = 0.58$ from Cepheids) to JWST observations, the authors resolve multiple anomalies—including the "Red Monster" efficiency crisis, the $z>8$ dust paradox, and the "Little Red Dot" black hole mass discrepancy—within a single unified framework.

## 2. Strengths

### 2.1 Rigorous Statistical Forensics
The manuscript has moved significantly beyond simple correlation mining. The inclusion of **Posterior Predictive Checks** (Section 3.8.2) is a standout feature, rigorously demonstrating that the mass-dust signal ($\rho=+0.56$) exceeds selection-induced priors by $4.0\sigma$. This effectively dismantles the primary counter-argument (that high-mass/high-dust galaxies are simply the only ones detectable).

### 2.2 Robustness Across Methodologies
The **Cross-Code Robustness** analysis (Section 3.10.1) provides critical validation. Demonstrating that the signal persists across Prospector/BEAGLE (UNCOVER), EAZY (CEERS), and LePhare (COSMOS-Web) ensures that the results are astrophysical, not artifacts of specific SED fitting assumptions.

### 2.3 Spatial Resolution of the Mechanism
The **Resolved Core Screening** analysis (Section 3.6.1) adds a crucial dimension to the evidence. The observation of *bluer cores* in massive galaxies ($\rho = -0.18$) is a counter-intuitive prediction unique to TEP (standard inside-out growth predicts redder cores). This successfully breaks the "mass proxy" degeneracy, as the signal depends on the potential gradient profile rather than total mass.

### 2.4 Unification of Phenomena
The paper successfully connects disparate anomalies:
*   **Stellar Mass:** Corrected by global $\Gamma_t$.
*   **Black Holes:** Corrected by differential shear ($\Gamma_t^{\rm cen} \gg \Gamma_t^{\rm halo}$).
*   **Dust:** Explained by extended effective time ($t_{\rm eff}$).
*   **Hubble Tension:** Linked via the same $\alpha_0$ parameter.
The ability to explain $M_{\rm BH}/M_* \sim 0.1$ and $M_* > M_{\rm dyn}$ with the same physics is a strong theoretical virtue.

## 3. Critique and Areas for Improvement

### 3.1 Theoretical Dependency
While Appendix A outlines the scalar-tensor derivation, the text relies heavily on the phenomenological $\Gamma_t$ formula. The manuscript would benefit from explicitly stating that the *cosmological* viability (BBN, CMB power spectrum) relies on the screening mechanisms established in Paper 1, as this remains the largest hurdle for any modified gravity theory.

### 3.2 Spectroscopic Sample Size
The spectroscopic validation ($N=147$) confirms the signal, but the bin-level statistics at $z>8$ ($N=32$) are still limited. The manuscript acknowledges this honestly, but it remains the primary empirical bottleneck compared to the photometric sample ($N>2,000$).

### 3.3 The "Standard Physics" Strawman
The comparison to standard physics is fair, particularly the AIC/BIC analysis. However, "bursty star formation" models are rapidly evolving. While TEP currently fits the data better (redder, dustier, older), future feedback models might reproduce some of these signatures without modified gravity. The "Falsifiability Roadmap" (Section 4.14) is excellent for addressing this future landscape.

## 4. Final Verdict
This is a high-quality, scientifically rigorous manuscript. It transitions TEP from a "provocative hypothesis" to a "statistically supported physical model." The tone is academic and measured, the evidence is triangulated from multiple independent angles, and the falsifiability criteria are clear.

**Recommendation:** **Strong Accept** for high-impact astronomy journals (e.g., ApJ, MNRAS, Nature Astronomy), provided the theoretical appendix remains robust.
