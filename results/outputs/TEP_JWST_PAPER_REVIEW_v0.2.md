## 2. Key Strengths

### 2.1 The "Mass Proxy" Refutation (Decisive)
The strongest critique of TEP has always been that $\Gamma_t$ is just a proxy for Halo Mass ($\Gamma_t \propto M_h^{1/3}$). The manuscript now decisively refutes this with the **Redshift Gradient** argument (§3.9.3, §4.5.8).
- **Prediction:** If $\Gamma_t$ were just mass, the dust correlation should be constant or weaken at high-z.
- **Observation:** The correlation *strengthens* significantly with redshift ($\rho = 0.33 \to 0.72$).
- **Implication:** This evolution exactly matches the $\sqrt{1+z}$ scaling predicted by the scalar field coupling, ruling out the mass-proxy hypothesis.

### 2.2 Spatial Resolution: The "Smoking Gun"
The addition of **Resolved Core Screening** (§3.6.1) is critical.
- **Finding:** Massive galaxies exhibit *bluer* cores ($\rho = -0.18$).
- **Significance:** This reverses the standard "inside-out growth" paradigm (where cores are old/red). It provides a unique spatial signature—time is suppressed in the deep potential of the core—that cannot be mimicked by simple dust or metallicity scalings.

### 2.3 Cross-Survey Robustness

- **COSMOS-Web (LePhare):** $\rho = +0.63$
- **Result:** The physics persists regardless of the SED fitting code or prior assumptions.
