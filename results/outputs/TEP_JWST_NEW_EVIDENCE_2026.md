# TEP-JWST: New Evidence & Reinterpretation (2026)

## 1. The "Little Red Dot" (LRD) Crisis: CAPERS-LRD-z9
**Discovery**: Taylor et al. (2025) report `CAPERS-LRD-z9` at $z=9.288$.
- **Observation**: Broad-line H$\alpha$ indicating a Supermassive Black Hole (SMBH).
- **Constraint**: Cosmic age at $z=9.3$ is $\sim 500$ Myr.
- **Anomaly**: Black hole mass requires Super-Eddington growth or massive seeds ($\sim 10^4 M_\odot$). Standard growth is too slow.

**TEP Reinterpretation**:
- **Mechanism**: The LRD represents a deep gravitational potential core.
- **Prediction**: Deep potential $\implies$ High Temporal Enhancement ($\Gamma_t \gg 1$).
- **Calculation**: If $\log M_h \sim 11-12$, TEP predicts $\Gamma_t \sim 3-5$.
- **Result**: The "effective" time available for growth is $t_{\rm eff} = \Gamma_t \times t_{\rm cosmic} \approx 4 \times 500 \text{ Myr} = 2 \text{ Gyr}$.
- **Conclusion**: A standard SMBH can easily form in 2 Gyr. The "Super-Eddington" requirement is an artifact of assuming $t_{\rm cosmic}$ applies to the deep interior of the potential.

## 4. The "Blue Core" Structural Inversion
**Discovery**: Jin et al. (2025) and Ju et al. (2025).
- **Observation**: At $z > 4$, galaxies frequently show **positive color gradients** (bluer centers, redder outskirts) and rising metallicity gradients. This is the opposite of the "inside-out" growth (redder centers) seen at $z < 2$.
- **Standard Interpretation**: Central starbursts or compaction events.
- **TEP Reinterpretation**: **Core Screening**.
    - **Core**: Deep potential $\implies$ Screening $\implies \Gamma_t \to 1$. Evolution is standard (young/blue at high z).
    - **Outskirts**: Shallower potential $\implies$ Unscreened $\implies \Gamma_t > 1$. Evolution is accelerated (appears older/redder).
    - **Result**: A "Blue Core / Red Outskirt" morphology is the direct prediction of differential screening in massive high-z halos.

## 5. The Stellar-Dynamical Mass Crisis ($M_* > M_{\rm dyn}$)
**Observation**: Multiple reports (e.g., de Graaff et al. 2024, Carnall et al.) of compact high-z galaxies where the inferred stellar mass $M_*$ approaches or exceeds the total dynamical mass $M_{\rm dyn}$.
- **Anomaly**: Physically impossible in standard $\Lambda$CDM (stars cannot exceed the total mass). Requires extreme assumptions (zero dark matter, specific IMFs) to fix.
- **TEP Resolution**:
    - $M_{\rm dyn}$ measures the true gravitational potential (invariant).
    - $M_*$ is inferred from luminosity using $M/L$ ratios.
    - **Mechanism**: TEP enhances the apparent age $\implies$ higher $M/L$ $\implies$ overestimated $M_*$.
    - **Prediction**: The ratio $M_*/M_{\rm dyn}$ should correlate with $\Gamma_t$. The violation is an artifact of the isochrony assumption.

## 6. The Quenching Timescale Crisis

## 7. The Gas-Poor Anomaly
**Observation**: High-z massive galaxies often appear "gas poor" or have extremely high Star Formation Efficiencies (SFE) / low gas depletion times ($t_{\rm dep} < 100$ Myr).
- **TEP Resolution**:
    - $SFE = SFR / M_{\rm gas}$.
    - $SFR$ is inferred from luminosity. $L \propto SFR$.
    - Under TEP, $L$ is standard (kinematic), but $M_{\rm gas}$ estimates (dynamical/CO) are physical.
    - However, if $M_*$ is inflated (see point 5), the *inferred* gas fraction $f_{\rm gas} = M_{\rm gas}/(M_{\rm gas}+M_*)$ is artificially suppressed.
    - **Conclusion**: The "gas poor" nature is a consequence of overestimated stellar masses.

**Discovery**: EPOCHS Paper X (2025).
- **Observation**: At $z > 4.5$, galaxies in overdense regions (protoclusters) have **higher sSFR** (0.0-0.5 dex) than field galaxies.
- **Anomaly**: At low redshift, dense environments *quench* star formation (lower sSFR). This "reversal" is unexpected in standard environmental quenching models (which usually predict quenching or no difference at high z).
- **TEP Reinterpretation**: **Group Halo Screening**.

    - **Conclusion**: The "suppressed" sSFR of field galaxies is the TEP signature. The protocluster galaxies are the "control" group where GR is restored.
