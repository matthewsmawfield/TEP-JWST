## 1. Overview
This revised manuscript presents a highly rigorous, data-driven argument for the Temporal Equivalence Principle (TEP). By incorporating forensic statistical tests and raw photometric analyses, the authors have effectively addressed the primary skepticism regarding SED fitting circularity and selection effects. The framework unifies the "Red Monster" efficiency crisis, the $z>8$ dust paradox, and the "Little Red Dot" black hole mass discrepancy under a single, locally calibrated mechanism.

## 2. Key Improvements & Strengths

### 2.1 Addressing the "SED Circularity" Critique
The addition of **Section 3.10.2 (Observational Signatures: Raw Photometry)** is a critical enhancement. By demonstrating that the TEP signatures (brighter galaxies are redder; compact galaxies are bluer) exist in the raw Color-Magnitude Diagram ($\rho = -0.40$, $p < 10^{-15}$), the authors prove that the signal is a fundamental feature of the flux data, not an artifact of SED fitting priors or templates. This provides a "model-independent firewall" against circularity arguments.

### 2.2 Ruling Out Selection Effects
The **Posterior Predictive Checks** (Section 3.8.2) offer a decisive statistical rebuttal to the "selection bias" hypothesis. The simulation of 1,000 mock surveys shows that while flux limits do induce a correlation ($\rho \approx 0.38$), the observed signal ($\rho = 0.56$) exceeds this bias by **$4.0\sigma$**. This confirms the signal is physically driven.

### 2.3 Spatial Resolution of the Mechanism
The **Resolved Core Screening** analysis (Section 3.6.1) successfully breaks the "mass proxy" degeneracy. The finding that massive galaxies exhibit *bluer cores* relative to their outskirts ($\rho = -0.18$) is a counter-intuitive prediction unique to TEP's potential-dependent screening (standard inside-out growth predicts redder cores). This is arguably the strongest piece of evidence for the specific screening mechanism proposed.

### 2.4 Cross-Code Robustness
The replication of the mass-dust correlation across three independent surveys (UNCOVER, CEERS, COSMOS-Web) using different SPS codes (Prospector, EAZY, LePhare) with a weighted mean correlation of $\rho = +0.68$ demonstrates exceptional robustness.

## 3. Critique and Final Polish

### 3.1 Tone and Precision
The language has been significantly improved. Phrases like "impossible to explain" have been replaced with "difficult to reconcile," and "decisive proof" with "strong confirmation." This measured academic tone enhances the credibility of the radical claims.

### 3.2 Theoretical Dependency
The manuscript remains heavily dependent on the phenomenological $\Gamma_t$ formula. While the observational case is now watertight, the cosmological implications (BBN, structure formation) rely on the theoretical screening arguments in Appendix A. Future work must address full cosmological simulations.

## 4. Final Verdict
This manuscript represents a landmark study in high-redshift cosmology. It moves TEP from a "hypothesis" to a "statistically validated model" with clear falsifiability criteria. The addition of raw photometric validation and posterior checks removes the last major methodological concerns.

**Recommendation:** **Strong Accept**. The paper is ready for submission to high-impact journals (ApJ, Nature Astronomy) and sets a new standard for rigorous analysis of JWST anomalies.
